{\footnotesize\ttfamily \mbox{\hyperlink{class_ray}{Ray}} Ortogonal\+Camera\+::\+Get\+Ray (\begin{DoxyParamCaption}\item[{\mbox{\hyperlink{struct_vector2}{Vector2}}}]{uv }\end{DoxyParamCaption})\hspace{0.3cm}{\ttfamily [virtual]}}

To generate the image in orthogonal (parallel) projection, we consider the view body that has a rectangular shape. This projection is suitable for depicting objects for which we want to avoid perspective distortion. To achieve that we are sending a ray from the center of each pixel of the projection plane toward the stage. The plane should be set to the center of the coordinate system. To locate the center of a pixel, perform a simple operation\+: \[ pixel\_width = \frac{2.0}{vertical\_image\_resolution} \\ pixel\_height = \frac{2.0}{horizontal\_image\_resolution} \] Having the size of the pixel it\textquotesingle{}s plausible to find it\textquotesingle{}s center in the coordinate system\+: \[ center\_x = -1.0 + (x + 0.5) * pixel\_width; \\ center\_y = 1.0 - (y + 0.5) * pixel\_height; \]
