The simplest example of texturing reproduces a rectangular texture $ (x, z) \in [-1, +1] x [-1, +1] $ on the (x,z) plane. Having a point on the plane (x, 0, z), coordinates, $ v $ can be calculated\+: \[ u = \frac{(z + 1)}{2} \\ v = \frac{(x - 1)}{2} \] Having the calculated intersection point of the coordinates (x, y, z) find the appropriate coordinate coordinates $ \phi $ and $ \theta $ assuming that the radius of such a sphere is $ r = 1 $ . Coordinate $ \phi $ will be the same as in the case of a cylinder, one should consider the value $ \theta $ . By using the running properties of the new coordinate system\+: $ \theta = \cos^{-1}(y) $. Where $ \theta = \cos^{-1}(y) $ can be represented as $ acos(y). acos(y) \in [0, \pi ] $ however, $ \theta = 0 $ when $ v = 1 $. Parameters (u, v) will be calculated\+: \[ u = \frac{ \phi }{2 \pi } \\ v = 1 - \frac{ \theta }{ \pi } \]
