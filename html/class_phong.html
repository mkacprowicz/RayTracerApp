Detailed Description

Phong class is a representation of the Phong model.

Phong model can be represented as given formula:

\[ I_{out} = I_{in}(k_d (L \cdot N ) k_s (R \cdot V)^{n} ) \]

where \(L\) is the vector towards the light source, \(V\) towards the observer, \(N\) is normal to the surface and \(R\) is the reflection direction. \(k_d\) and \(k_s\) respectively, reflection coefficients and diffuse reflection coefficient, \(n\) reflection flare. As we are currently considering only direct lighting, we will save the Phong lighting model as:

\[ L_{o} = L_{i}(k_d (L \cdot N ) k_s (R \cdot V)^{n} ) \]

where \(L_o\) is the intensity reflected to the observer and \(L_i\) is the intensity from the light source. By writing this equation using the BRDF function, we get:

\[ L_o = L_i Refl(w_i, w_o) \\ L_o = Refl(w_i, w_o)L_i \\ L_o = \frac{cos\theta_i dw_i}{cos\theta_i dw_i} Refl(w_i, w_o)L_i \\ L_o = \frac{Refl(w_i, w_o)}{cos\theta_i dw_i}L_i cos\theta_i dw_i \]

The Phong lighting model can be considered as a special case of BRDF, used for materials with reflective properties approximated by:

\[ BRDF(\theta_i, \phi_i, \theta_o, \phi_o) = \frac{Refl(w_i, w_o)}{cos\theta_i dw_i} \\ BRDF(\theta_i, \phi_i, \theta_o, \phi_o) = \frac{k_d (w_i \times N) + k_s (R \times w_o)^n}{cos\theta_i dw_i} \]
