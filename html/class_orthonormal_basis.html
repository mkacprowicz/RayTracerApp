We use orthonormal bases (ONB) in raytracing wherever we need a local coordinate system. The ONB can be constructed from any two vectors \(a\) and \(b\) (they need not be mutually perpendicular or normalized). The first step is to create a vector \(w\) - it is a normalized vector parallel to \(a\). Then the vector \(u\) is calculated as the normalized vector product \(b \times w\). Ultimately v is equal \(w \times u\) thus creating a clockwise coordinate system:

\[ w = \frac{a}{||a||} \\ u = \frac{(b \times w)}{||b \times w||} \\ v = w \times u \]
